# M31.4 — River water rim lock

## Problem

M31.3 shaped the symmetric channel correctly, but vanilla water was still allowed to spread horizontally from the explicitly filled atlas channel. A source block could move onto a solid ledge outside the channel mask and then fall down as a wide waterfall curtain. Visually this made the water surface appear many blocks above the gravel river rim even though the fitted channel itself was lower.

## Fix

- Atlas channel water no longer uses vanilla horizontal spread. Every channel column is already filled directly with source blocks after carving, so spreading is unnecessary.
- The lower sampled bank is now a hard ceiling for the cooked water profile. The builder uses the minimum valid bank support probe instead of a lower-third percentile.
- Runtime section quantisation is one block along the centerline instead of four blocks, so a steep local rim cannot inherit a higher water level from a nearby section.
- `waterBelowBankBlocks` is restricted to `0..1`; the default remains `1`.
- Surface polish removes stale non-source flowing water up to `riverOverflowCleanupBlocks` outside old river banks. The default repair distance is 16 blocks.
- Cooked river cache fingerprint changed to `river-profile-rim-locked-no-spread-v6`.

## Required retest

```mcfunction
/rla reload
/rla finish_here 4
```

For an uncontaminated visual result, use new chunks. `/rla reload` rebuilds the old `region-cache.rriver` automatically.
